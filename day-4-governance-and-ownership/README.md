# Day 4 — Governance and Ownership

## Central Question

Who owns infrastructure after it is provisioned, and how do organizations safely operate infrastructure at scale without sacrificing speed, governance, or accountability?

---

## Why This Matters

Infrastructure does not stop evolving after it is created.

As organizations grow, they face challenges such as:

* Multiple engineering teams
* Shared infrastructure platforms
* Governance requirements
* Security standards
* Operational accountability
* Infrastructure ownership

Without clear ownership and governance, infrastructure becomes difficult to manage, audit, and evolve safely.

Platform Engineering addresses these challenges by combining standardization, governance, automation, and self-service capabilities.

---

## Platform Engineering Angle

Platform Engineering decentralizes infrastructure consumption without decentralizing infrastructure ownership.

Platform teams provide:

* Golden paths
* Standardized modules
* Platform APIs
* Governance controls
* Security controls
* Operational guardrails

Application teams consume those capabilities to build and operate business services.

This enables safe self-service while maintaining consistency across the organization.

---

## Key Message

As organizations scale, the challenge shifts from provisioning infrastructure to governing how infrastructure is consumed.

The most successful platforms provide self-service capabilities while maintaining clear ownership boundaries, automated governance, and operational accountability.

Platform Engineering enables teams to move fast without creating operational chaos.
